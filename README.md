# AGGREGATE CPI IS A FICTION FOR ANY SPECIFIC FAMILY

India’s headline inflation print sits in the low single digits in most months. But that number is a weighted blend of food, fuel, clothing and a thin housing line - a basket that belongs to no one in particular. The inflation a household experiences is set by the one or two goals it is saving for, and those goals run on completely different clocks.

A family funding a four-year engineering degree experiences education inflation closer to ~11%, compounding against a headline of ~5%. A couple planning a parent’s healthcare faces medical inflation near ~14%. A young household saving for a first home tracks a residential price curve that the official basket barely registers, because housing enters CPI as a single under-weighted sub-group. The gap between the headline and the lived rate is where retirement plans quietly fail.
Every Financial Planner already teaches goal-bucketing - education, healthcare, housing, lifestyle. Nobody has productised it. There is no listed instrument an Indian investor can buy to hedge the specific inflation their goal is exposed to. That is the white space we had to fill.

We chose Education CPI for our research
